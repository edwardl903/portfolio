My Musical Journey

My piano journey has been quite the ride. I started at 7, but after a couple of years, I quit - convinced I just wasn't cut out for it. It's funny how we can be so certain about things at that age. But at 13, I decided to give it another shot and started taking lessons again. That's when everything changed.

Having a great teacher made all the difference. I rediscovered my love for music and actually started enjoying the process of learning. I even had the chance to perform at a few retirement homes, which was nerve-wracking but incredibly rewarding. Those experiences helped me build confidence and realize that sometimes our biggest obstacles are the ones we create for ourselves.

These days, I love exploring all sorts of musical styles - from classical and jazz to pop, movie soundtracks, and oriental music. I enjoy coming up with my own compositions too. There's something really special about sitting down at the piano and just playing whatever comes to mind. It's become such an important part of who I am.

Looking back, I'm grateful I gave piano a second chance. It taught me that it's never too late to rediscover a passion, and that persistence can turn even the most challenging beginnings into something meaningful. Sometimes the best things in life come from being willing to try again.
